Knowledge Base & FAQs

Commonly asked questions regarding Zakat calculation, Shariah principles, and how Al-Hisab works.

What is the current Nisab for Zakat in 2026?

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before they are obligated to pay Zakat. It is based on the value of 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver. Al-Hisab automatically updates these values based on live market prices to give you an accurate calculation.

Is Al-Hisab's calculation Shariah compliant?

Yes, all our calculators are designed based on established Fiqh principles. We consult historical Shariah standards and contemporary scholarly opinions to ensure that complex assets like Crypto and Stocks are calculated accurately.

Do I have to pay Zakat on Cryptocurrency?

Most scholars agree that if Cryptocurrency is held as an investment or for trading, it is subject to Zakat at a rate of 2.5% of its current market value, provided it meets the Nisab and the lunar year requirement.

Is my financial data stored on your servers?

Absolutely not. Al-Hisab operates on a local processing model. All the numbers you enter stay on your browser and are never sent to or stored on our databases. Your privacy is our priority.

How often are the market rates updated?

We use high-speed APIs that refresh gold, silver, and digital asset prices multiple times per day to ensure your Zakat calculation is as precise as possible at the time of calculation.
