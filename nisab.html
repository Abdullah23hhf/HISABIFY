Understanding Zakat Nisab in 2026

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam, a mandatory charitable contribution that serves as a system of wealth redistribution and purification. However, Zakat is not due on all wealth; it is only required when your qualifying assets exceed a specific threshold known as the **Nisab**. This terminal provides real-time data to help you determine that threshold based on current global market conditions.

How Nisab is Calculated

The Nisab was set by the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) at a rate equivalent to 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver. In the modern financial era, the value of these metals fluctuates daily. Our terminal tracks these fluctuations to provide you with an exact figure in your local currency.

  • Gold Nisab: Traditionally used for those who primarily hold gold or diversified assets.
  • Silver Nisab: Often recommended by scholars as it sets a lower threshold, allowing more benefit for the poor.
  • Cash & Savings: If your cash balance exceeds the silver nisab, Zakat becomes mandatory.

The Condition of 'Hawl'

Possessing wealth above the Nisab is only the first step. The second condition is the **Hawl**, which means you must have maintained that level of wealth for one full lunar year. If your wealth dips below the Nisab during the year, the countdown resets.

Qualifying Assets for Zakat

Determining what to include in your Zakat calculation is crucial. Not all possessions are subject to Zakat. Generally, assets that are "productive" or have the potential for growth are included.

1. Personal Gold and Silver

All gold and silver jewelry, coins, and bullion are subject to Zakat. Many scholars agree that even jewelry used for personal adornment should be included if it reaches the Nisab. White gold and platinum follow different rules, but the core focus remains on the gold content within them.

2. Cash and Bank Balances

Any money held in current accounts, savings accounts, or kept as physical cash at home must be totaled. This includes money set aside for future goals like a wedding or Hajj, as long as it is currently in your possession.

3. Stocks and Investments

Shares held for the purpose of trade are zakatable at their full market value. If shares are held as long-term investments for dividends, Zakat is calculated on the zakatable assets of the company (usually found in their balance sheet).

4. Cryptocurrencies & Digital Assets

In the digital age, Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other crypto-assets are considered wealth. Since they have a market value and can be traded, they are included in your total wealth calculation at their current market price at the time of your Zakat date.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I pay my Zakat?

Zakat is due once your wealth has been above the Nisab for a full lunar year. Many people choose to pay during Ramadan for extra blessings, but your Zakat anniversary is unique to when you first met the Nisab threshold.

Can I subtract my debts?

Yes, short-term debts or the immediate installment of a long-term loan can be deducted from your total wealth before comparing it to the Nisab.

What if the Gold and Silver Nisab give different values?

Currently, the value of 612g of silver is much lower than 87g of gold. Following the Silver Nisab is more precautionary as it ensures the poor receive more help. However, following the Gold Nisab is also valid according to many schools of thought.
